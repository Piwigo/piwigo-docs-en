# Customization options for your gallery

There are various ways to customize your Piwigo gallery:

- By choosing and setting up the [theme](../piwigo-themes/index.md) of your choice
- By installing [plugins](../piwigo-plugins/index.md)
- But Piwigo's different standard configuration options should also be explored. This is what we are showcasing in this article.

For this, go to the administration, in the Configuration > Options menu. This menu centralizes various customization options for your Piwigo gallery.

## General tab

The first tab is divided into several sections.

### Basic settings

![Paramètres de base.jpg](https://ressources.piwigo.com/uploads/c/v/7/cv7jpz6hf8/2026/07/03/20260703190344-6aa7ad20.jpg)

**Editing your gallery’s title**

Here, you can choose your gallery's title: in a way, this is the name of your website. It is used by Piwigo in multiple places.

Some themes, such as Bootstrap Darkroom, show the gallery's title on all pages by default (as you can see below), but this is not the case with Modus, the default theme.

![Changement de titre.jpg](https://ressources.piwigo.com/uploads/c/v/7/cv7jpz6hf8/2026/07/03/20260703190344-bc3dc9ac.jpg)

**Setting up the content of a banner**

The "Page banner" area lets you create a banner which will be displayed at the top of all pages on your website just like the example below.

![Bannière Modus.jpg](https://ressources.piwigo.com/uploads/c/v/7/cv7jpz6hf8/2026/07/03/20260703190344-2cd18d15.jpg)

To learn more about creating banners for your Piwigo gallery, read this article:

[Adding a custom banner to your gallery](adding-a-custom-banner-to-your-gallery.md)

**Changing the photos' display order**

This is also where you can modify the order in which photos are displayed on pages. By default, they are sorted by upload date (from newest to oldest), then in alphabetical order and by ID, but you can customize this display order.

### Permissions

![Liste permissions.jpg](https://ressources.piwigo.com/uploads/c/v/7/cv7jpz6hf8/2026/07/03/20260703190344-2d8c0809.jpg)

This is where you can choose whether or not to activate ratings on your gallery ("Allow rating" option). [Learn more about ratings](../comments-and-ratings/managing-ratings-votes.md)

This is also where you will choose whether or not you allow users to be able to create an account on their own ("Allow user registration" option). [Learn more about users](../managing-users/creating-and-managing-users.md)

Lastly, here, you can choose whether or not to allow users to customize their display preferences from the gallery (by activating or not activating the "Customize" menu). [Learn more about preferences](../managing-users/creating-and-managing-users.md)

### Miscellaneous

![Aléatoires.jpg](https://ressources.piwigo.com/uploads/c/v/7/cv7jpz6hf8/2026/07/03/20260703190344-c50ff7a8.jpg)

The options available in the Miscellaneous section aren't really related to gallery customization, but rather general options applied to Piwigo as a whole:

- first day of the week
- saving visit history
- whether or not to promote the mobile app
- theme for the emails sent (light or dark)
- whether or not to detect duplicates during upload

## Watermark tab

This tab lets you automatically display a watermark on all photos displayed in your gallery.

## Display tab

This tab lets you customize your gallery with many options you can activate or deactivate.

It is divided into multiple sections.

### Main page

![Options page principale.jpg](https://ressources.piwigo.com/uploads/c/v/7/cv7jpz6hf8/2026/07/03/20260703190344-22be9496.jpg)

The "Main page" section contains various options for customizing the actions available in your gallery.

Namely, this is where you can customize the number of albums per page in your gallery. By default, this number is set to 12, but if you have a lot of albums, you can display more of them.

This is also where you will be able to activate or deactivate most of the icons available on your gallery's home page, or on an album page.

### Photo page

The "Photo page" section contains the actions and display options you can activate or deactivate on a photo page.

![Options page photo.jpg](https://ressources.piwigo.com/uploads/c/v/7/cv7jpz6hf8/2026/07/03/20260703190344-88cafede.jpg)

This way, you can customize the actions available for a photo, namely activating or deactivating the download.

You can also choose whether or not to activate the navigation bar that lets you go through the photos of an album.

Lastly, you can choose whether or not to display the gallery menu from a photo page (it is hidden by default).

### Photo properties

The "Photo properties" section lets you choose the properties to display in the information section on a photo page.

![Propriétés photo.jpg](https://ressources.piwigo.com/uploads/c/v/7/cv7jpz6hf8/2026/07/03/20260703190344-959b6a38.jpg)

[Learn more about properties](../import-and-manage-photos/properties-metadata-photos-piwigo.md)

## Comments tab

This tab lets you activate or deactivate comments on your gallery, and set up some options related to comments.

[Learn more about comments](../comments-and-ratings/managing-comments.md)

## Customizing your gallery's appearance through preferences

There is another possibility, often unknown, which lets you edit some display settings of your gallery: these are the user preferences.

For example, preferences let you customize:

- The number of photos per page on an album
- The display period through the "Recent photos" menu
- The display of sub-albums in a tree structure in the browsing menu
- etc.

Preferences can be different for each user, but, as an administrator, you can:

- Set the default preferences for each new user
- Set the preferences for the "guest" user (anonymous users of your gallery, not logged in)
- Edit your own preferences and those of other users.

[Learn more about preferences](../managing-users/creating-and-managing-users.md)
